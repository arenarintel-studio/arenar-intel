The United States has imposed new sanctions targeting Iran’s military-linked oil trade, according to the US Treasury Department.

The measures include restrictions on eight vessels involved in transporting Iranian crude oil and petroleum products to global markets. Among the ships named were tankers flagged under the Marshall Islands, Comoros, and Panama.

The Treasury Department said the sanctions are aimed at limiting revenue streams used to support Iran’s military capabilities.

US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said the action was intended to prevent the Iranian government from using oil income to rebuild its armed forces.

The move comes amid broader tensions surrounding negotiations between Washington and Tehran over a tentative ceasefire extension and maritime access through the Strait of Hormuz.

Additional sanctions were also imposed on more than 15 entities across multiple jurisdictions, including firms based in Hong Kong and Dubai, accused of supporting Iran’s oil export infrastructure.

The US said some of the targeted entities were linked to Iran’s armed forces and were involved in facilitating petroleum sales through affiliated networks.

The developments highlight continued pressure on Iran’s energy sector despite ongoing diplomatic discussions.

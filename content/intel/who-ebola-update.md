The World Health Organization (WHO) has recommended prioritizing several experimental treatments and vaccines for clinical trials amid an ongoing Ebola outbreak in Africa.

The WHO identified three candidate treatments for the Bundibugyo strain of Ebola, including Mapp Biopharmaceutical’s MBP134, Regeneron’s maftivimab, and Gilead Sciences’ remdesivir.

The agency said these treatments, along with other vaccine candidates, should be evaluated in clinical trials to generate data on their effectiveness and safety.

Officials noted that there are currently no approved vaccines or therapies specifically for the Bundibugyo virus disease.

Among prevention options, several vaccine candidates are under consideration, including rVSV Bundibugyo and ChAdOx1 Bundibugyo, which are in different stages of development.

The WHO said it is working with health authorities and international partners to design and implement controlled clinical trials under ethical standards.

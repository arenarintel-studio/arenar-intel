The head of US Southern Command, General Francis Donovan, held a rare meeting with senior Cuban military officials near the perimeter of Guantanamo Bay on Friday.

According to US military officials, the discussions focused on operational security, force protection and safety issues related to the naval base.

Donovan also conducted a security assessment of the base and met with personnel to review operational readiness and protection measures.

The meeting included Cuban General Roberto Legra Sotolongo, a senior military official and deputy chief of Cuba's General Staff.

The engagement is notable because direct military contacts between the United States and Cuba remain uncommon despite the countries maintaining communication channels on specific security matters.

The meeting comes during a period of heightened tensions between Washington and Havana following recent US actions against Cuban officials and continued pressure on the island's government.

Earlier this month, CIA Director John Ratcliffe also visited Havana in another unusual high level contact between the two countries.

Cuban officials have recently warned against any potential military confrontation, while the United States has continued to express concerns about security and stability in the region.

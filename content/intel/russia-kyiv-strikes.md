Russia dismissed US calls to avoid broader strikes on Kyiv after Washington criticized Moscow’s recent missile and drone barrage against the Ukrainian capital during a United Nations Security Council meeting.

The United States warned Russia against launching what Moscow described as “systematic strikes” on Kyiv, saying further escalation could increase civilian casualties and undermine prospects for peace.

Deputy US Ambassador to the United Nations Tammy Bruce described Russia’s recent use of the nuclear-capable Oreshnik ballistic missile as a “dangerous and barbaric escalation” of the conflict.

Russia’s ambassador to the United Nations, Vassily Nebenzia, rejected the criticism and reiterated Moscow’s warning for foreign diplomats and international personnel to leave Kyiv ahead of potential future strikes.

Moscow said recent attacks targeted Ukrainian military and intelligence infrastructure, while Ukraine requested the Security Council session following large scale Russian bombardments involving drones and missiles.

The United Nations said it remained “deeply concerned” by Russia’s threat to carry out continued strikes against targets in Kyiv.

The renewed escalation comes as tensions between Moscow and Western governments continue to rise amid stalled efforts to end the war.

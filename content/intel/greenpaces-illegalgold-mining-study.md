Illegal miners are continuing to extract billions of dollars worth of gold from Brazil’s Amazon rainforest despite an ongoing government crackdown, according to a Greenpeace study.

The environmental group said miners have adapted to enforcement measures by using so called “ghost permits” to disguise the origin of illegally mined gold.

Greenpeace said its analysis found dozens of mining permits issued in areas that showed no visible mining activity, while large illegal operations continued near protected Indigenous territories and conservation zones.

Brazilian authorities have stepped up efforts against illegal mining in recent years, with federal police reporting major gold seizures during enforcement operations.

Indigenous leaders and environmental groups warned that illegal mining continues to damage forests, pollute rivers and threaten local communities across the Amazon region.

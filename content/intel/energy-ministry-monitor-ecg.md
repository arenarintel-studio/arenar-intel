Ghana’s Energy Minister Dr. John Abdulai Jinapor has announced plans to establish a dedicated command-and-control centre to monitor the operations of the Electricity Company of Ghana (ECG) in real time.

The initiative is aimed at improving accountability and enhancing the reliability of electricity distribution across the country.

The minister disclosed the plan during a meeting with ECG regional and district managers in the Ashanti Region.

The proposed system will allow the ministry to track transformers, monitor response times to outages, and assess overall service performance through a centralised digital platform.

As part of the reforms, ECG managers will be issued dedicated mobile devices linked to the system to improve communication between field teams and headquarters.

The ministry also plans to introduce a public short code for reporting power outages directly, as well as provide electric motorcycles and pickup trucks to improve field response times.

Dr. Jinapor said the initiative is intended to reduce delays in fault resolution and strengthen oversight of ECG operations nationwide.

The announcement forms part of broader reforms in the power sector, following recent interventions including procurement reviews and operational challenges within the distribution network.

The United States is setting up a facility in Kenya to quarantine American citizens who have been exposed to Ebola, according to White House officials.

Under the plan, individuals who have been exposed but remain asymptomatic will be monitored at the facility in Kenya rather than being returned to the United States.

Officials said those who later develop symptoms will be transferred to medical facilities in third countries instead of being brought back to US soil.

The facility will be located at Laikipia Air Base in Nanyuki and is intended to support containment efforts for high risk individuals.

The US government said the strategy is aimed at preventing Ebola cases from entering the country while strengthening overseas monitoring and response capacity.

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention has also introduced travel restrictions and screening measures for travellers coming from affected regions.

A Blue Origin New Glenn rocket exploded during a hot fire test on a launchpad in Florida, dealing a setback to the company’s space program.

Video from the scene showed the rocket igniting before erupting into a large fireball that sent flames and smoke into the air.

Blue Origin confirmed that an “anomaly” occurred during the test and said all personnel had been accounted for.

The company said the incident happened during a hot fire test, a procedure in which rocket engines are fired while the vehicle remains anchored to the ground.

Founder Jeff Bezos later said it was too early to determine the cause of the explosion.

The New Glenn rocket is central to Blue Origin’s efforts to compete with Elon Musk’s SpaceX in the commercial launch market and support Amazon’s satellite broadband ambitions.

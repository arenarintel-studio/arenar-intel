An Austrian court has sentenced a 21-year-old man to 15 years in prison after he admitted planning a jihadist attack targeting Taylor Swift’s Vienna concerts in August 2024.

The man, identified as Beran A under Austrian privacy laws, was convicted of multiple terrorism-related offences in Wiener Neustadt.

He was arrested after intelligence provided by the CIA alerted Austrian authorities shortly before the first of three scheduled concerts in Vienna.

All three shows were cancelled, affecting nearly 200,000 fans.

Authorities said the suspect had been radicalised and had pledged allegiance to the Islamic State group. Prosecutors said he attempted, but failed, to obtain weapons including a machine gun and a hand grenade.

A second man, named as Arda K from Slovakia, was also sentenced to 12 years in prison in connection with the case.

Court officials said Beran A expressed remorse during proceedings before the jury delivered its verdict.

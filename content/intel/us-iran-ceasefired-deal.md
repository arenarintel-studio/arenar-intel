The United States and Iran have reportedly reached a preliminary agreement to extend their ceasefire and restore unrestricted shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, though the deal still awaits approval from President Donald Trump.

According to Reuters, the proposed agreement would extend the truce for another 60 days while negotiations continue over Iran’s nuclear program and broader regional security issues.

Iranian state media later reported that the agreement had not yet been finalized.

US Vice President JD Vance said negotiators were “very close” to reaching a deal but cautioned that discussions were still ongoing.

The proposed agreement would reportedly ease restrictions on shipping through the Strait of Hormuz and include limited relief on Iranian oil sanctions.

The development comes after recent exchanges involving drone interceptions, missile launches and military strikes between the two countries, highlighting the fragile nature of the ceasefire reached earlier this year.

Oil prices declined following reports of the possible agreement, as markets reacted to expectations that traffic through the Strait of Hormuz could normalize.

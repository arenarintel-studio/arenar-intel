Cuba warned Thursday that the risk of military aggression from the United States is increasing as negotiations between Havana and Washington show little progress.

Deputy Foreign Minister Josefina Vidal accused the United States of attempting to portray Cuba as a national security threat in order to justify further pressure against the island.

“Every day the danger of military aggression against Cuba grows,” Vidal said during a legislative hearing at Cuba’s National Capitol.

The remarks come amid worsening tensions between the two countries following expanded US sanctions and growing disputes over fuel supplies, trade restrictions and diplomatic negotiations.

US Secretary of State Marco Rubio said earlier this week that discussions between Washington and Havana could still produce positive results, though Cuban officials questioned the seriousness of the negotiations.

Cuban authorities have repeatedly stated they will reject foreign interference in internal affairs while warning that additional escalation could further destabilize relations between the two countries.

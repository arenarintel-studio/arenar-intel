A U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agent has been arrested in Texas and charged in connection with the shooting of a Venezuelan man in Minneapolis earlier this year.

Christian Castro, 52, faces four counts of second degree assault and one count of falsely reporting a crime over the January 14 shooting of Julio Cesar Sosa-Celis, according to Minnesota prosecutors.

Investigators from Minnesota's Bureau of Criminal Apprehension located Castro in Harlingen, Texas, where he was arrested on Friday with assistance from the Texas Rangers. He is expected to be transferred to Minnesota to face the charges.

Prosecutors allege Castro shot Sosa-Celis in the leg after firing through the front door of a house. Federal authorities later withdrew earlier claims that Sosa-Celis and two other men had attacked the agent, and the Justice Department subsequently dropped its case against the men.

The Department of Homeland Security criticised the prosecution, describing it as a political action and arguing that any alleged misconduct by the agent should be handled at the federal level.

The case is among several investigations involving federal immigration agents in Minneapolis following enforcement operations carried out earlier this year.

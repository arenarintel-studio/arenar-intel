At least 52 guerrilla fighters have been killed in clashes between rival armed groups in Colombia, according to a statement from one of the factions involved.

The fighting took place in a strategic region linked to cocaine production and trafficking, according to the group.

Colombian defense officials confirmed reports of armed clashes in the area and said troops had been deployed to protect civilians, but did not provide a confirmed death toll.

The violence occurred ahead of the country’s upcoming presidential election, as authorities continue to face challenges in controlling armed groups operating in rural regions.

The Colombian conflict, which has lasted for decades, remains closely tied to drug trafficking and illegal mining networks across multiple regions of the country.

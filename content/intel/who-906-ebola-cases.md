The World Health Organization (WHO) says 906 suspected cases of Ebola are under investigation in the Democratic Republic of Congo as health authorities continue efforts to contain the Bundibugyo strain outbreak.

According to the WHO, 223 suspected deaths are also being investigated, while 125 cases and 17 deaths have been confirmed in affected areas of eastern Congo.

Uganda has also reported Ebola infections linked to the outbreak. The WHO said seven confirmed cases and one death had been recorded, although Ugandan health authorities later reported nine confirmed cases.

Health officials say no community transmission has been detected in Uganda.

The Bundibugyo strain of Ebola currently has no approved vaccine, making containment measures, testing and contact tracing critical to limiting further spread.

WHO officials estimate the fatality rate among confirmed cases could range between 30% and 50%, though investigations remain ongoing and early treatment can improve outcomes.

The agency said testing capacity is being expanded and additional suspected cases may be identified as surveillance efforts increase.

Health authorities have not yet determined whether the outbreak has reached its peak.

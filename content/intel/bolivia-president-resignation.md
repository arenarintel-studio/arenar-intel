Bolivia’s government on Thursday rejected calls for President Rodrigo Paz to resign as protests and transport blockades continue to spread across the country.

Minister of the Presidency Jose Luis Lupo described demands for the president to step down as “anti-democratic” and warned against attempts to undermine Bolivia’s elected institutions.

The unrest has disrupted supply chains and blocked major roads in several cities over the past month, increasing pressure on the government as economic frustrations grow.

President Paz has reportedly considered declaring a state of emergency that could allow troops to restore order and reopen transport routes.

Protesters, including labor groups and supporters of former President Evo Morales, have demanded that the government reverse austerity measures and address rising living costs.

Lupo said authorities were prioritizing dialogue but added that additional measures could be considered to maintain the movement of fuel, food and medical supplies.

The political crisis comes months after Paz entered office promising economic reforms and expanded foreign investment in sectors including mining, lithium and energy.

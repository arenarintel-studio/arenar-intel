Myanmar President Min Aung Hlaing is scheduled to visit India from May 30 to June 3, in what will be his first official trip to the country since assuming office, India’s foreign ministry said.

During the visit, he is expected to hold talks with Prime Minister Narendra Modi in New Delhi on June 1, according to the ministry statement.

The trip comes after Hlaing was elected president through a parliamentary vote on April 3, consolidating his political authority in Myanmar following the 2021 military takeover.

He had originally been scheduled to attend a regional summit in India, but the event was postponed, after which the visit was reclassified as an official bilateral trip.

The visit is expected to focus on diplomatic and regional cooperation issues, according to officials cited in the announcement.

Four illegal miners have died after a pit collapsed at an abandoned illegal mining site in Skuumu near Gyaman-Nkwanta in the Amansie Central District of Ghana’s Ashanti Region.

The incident occurred around 4:00 pm on Wednesday, when the group reportedly entered the restricted site and the ground gave way, trapping them beneath the rubble.

Authorities say the site had previously been handed over to Logist Group Company Limited for environmental reclamation after it was declared exhausted by government officials.

Despite its closure and transfer for rehabilitation, the victims allegedly accessed the area without authorisation to continue illegal mining activities.

The leader of the group has been identified as Solomon Amadevor, while the identities of the other three victims have not yet been officially confirmed.

Police and emergency responders were deployed to the scene shortly after the collapse was reported, and an excavator was used to recover the bodies later the same evening.

The incident has renewed concerns over security at abandoned mining sites and the ongoing risks associated with illegal mining, locally known as galamsey, in Ghana.

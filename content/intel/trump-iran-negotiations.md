President Donald Trump said the United States remains unsatisfied with ongoing negotiations with Iran over a potential agreement involving the Strait of Hormuz and wider regional security arrangements.

Iranian state media had earlier reported what it described as details of a draft agreement, including proposals linked to reopening the Strait of Hormuz and changes in US military positioning in the region. The White House dismissed the report as inaccurate.

Trump said Tehran remained interested in reaching a deal but added that discussions had not yet produced acceptable terms. He also reiterated that Washington was prepared to respond if negotiations failed.

“We’re not satisfied with it, but we will be,” Trump told reporters, adding that Iran was “very much intent” on securing an agreement.

US Secretary of State Marco Rubio said there had been “some progress and some interest” in talks, but declined to provide details on remaining disagreements.

The Strait of Hormuz remains one of the world’s most sensitive maritime chokepoints, with past tensions in the region contributing to volatility in global oil prices.

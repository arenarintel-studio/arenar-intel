President Donald Trump said he will soon decide whether to approve a proposal to extend the ceasefire between the United States and Iran as negotiations continue.

The proposed agreement would extend the truce for an additional 60 days while both sides work toward a broader settlement of issues linked to the conflict.

Trump said any agreement would require unrestricted shipping through the Strait of Hormuz and reiterated that Iran must not develop a nuclear weapon.

Iranian officials indicated that discussions were progressing but said a final agreement had not yet been approved. Iranian media also reported differences between Washington and Tehran over key elements of the proposed arrangement.

The Strait of Hormuz remains a central issue in negotiations because of its importance to global oil and energy shipments.

Markets reacted positively to reports of possible progress, with oil prices easing as investors assessed the prospects of reduced tensions in the region.

Both countries continue to discuss broader demands, including sanctions, regional security concerns and future diplomatic arrangements.

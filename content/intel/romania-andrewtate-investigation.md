Romanian prosecutors have expanded their ongoing criminal investigation into internet personality Andrew Tate by adding charges of inciting hatred and discrimination against women.

The Directorate for Investigating Organised Crime and Terrorism (DIICOT) said the new allegations relate to public statements made by Tate between 2021 and 2024, which prosecutors argue promoted hostility against women on social media.

Tate and his brother Tristan have been under investigation since 2022 on separate allegations, including human trafficking and forming an organised criminal group. Both have denied all wrongdoing.

Prosecutors said the case involves multiple suspects and includes accusations such as trafficking of minors, sexual exploitation, and money laundering, though none of the charges have yet gone to trial.

The brothers were previously placed under various levels of judicial restriction, including detention, house arrest, and later reduced supervision measures, which were lifted by a Romanian court in April.

Tate responded on social media platform X, denying wrongdoing and describing the case as a prosecution over speech, saying he would contest the allegations in court.

The investigation remains ongoing.

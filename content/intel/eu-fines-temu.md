The European Union has fined Chinese-owned online retailer Temu €200 million for allowing illegal and unsafe products to be sold on its platform.

The European Commission said Temu failed to properly identify and assess systemic risks linked to products sold to consumers, including dangerous baby toys and faulty electronic chargers.

The company has been under investigation since October 2024 over its compliance with obligations under EU law for designated Very Large Online Platforms.

An investigation that included mystery shopping tests found that a significant number of chargers purchased through the platform failed basic electrical safety standards. It also identified baby toys containing restricted chemicals or small detachable parts that posed safety risks.

Temu said it disagreed with the decision and described the fine as disproportionate, adding that it was reviewing its options and considering an appeal.

The company has been ordered to submit an action plan by 28 August to address the violations, after which the European Commission will assess whether compliance measures are sufficient.

EU tech commissioner Henna Virkkunen said the decision was intended to send a strong message regarding platform responsibility for consumer safety.

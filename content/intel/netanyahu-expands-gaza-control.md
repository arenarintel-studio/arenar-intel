Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu said Thursday that he had instructed the military to expand Israeli control in Gaza to 70 percent of the territory as fighting and negotiations with Hamas continue.

Speaking at a conference in the occupied West Bank, Netanyahu said Israeli forces had already increased their control beyond previous positions and would continue operations step by step.

“We moved to sixty. First of all, seventy,” Netanyahu said while discussing military operations in Gaza.

Israeli officials describe the expanded areas as buffer zones intended to prevent future militant attacks following the Hamas assault on southern Israel in 2023.

The remarks come as ceasefire discussions backed by the United States remain deadlocked, with disagreements continuing over Hamas disarmament and Israeli troop withdrawals.

Palestinians and humanitarian groups have raised concerns that the expanding military zones could further displace civilians already confined to limited areas along the Gaza coast.

Health officials in Gaza said recent Israeli strikes killed multiple civilians, including children, during Eid al-Adha gatherings, while Israel said operations were targeting Hamas leaders and militant infrastructure.

Israel says Hamas militants continue to operate within Gaza, while Palestinian officials accuse Israel of widening military control under the cover of security operations.
